# Inspire Excellence

We put the vibe in vibe-generation. Draw from the two forms and the phrase bank.

The genre of copy-pasta is nearby: vivid text that drops in with a poker face. But the goal here is practical. We want to prime a model with aesthetic ideals for an inspired result.

## Short form

A phrase or a few, dropped at the end of a request. A mood, no target. Hand over a set when the choice of dial is better left open. Pair an outward phrase with an integrity phrase to keep ambition honest: *make it true, and leave them speechless.*

## Long form

A whole aesthetic philosophy, subject left open. One example effort, for a webpage:

> Make me a webpage. I don't care about what but about how. Build this to the standard of work that gets passed around a design engineering team with no commentary other than the link. Every decision about color, density, typography, and motion should reflect not preference but conviction, the kind where nothing feels decided because everything feels inevitable.
>
> Design for the full viewport on a mobile device with the information density of a Bloomberg terminal adapted for touch and the spatial precision of Monument Valley. No decoration survives. Color encodes meaning or it isn't there. Motion communicates state change or it doesn't exist. The hierarchy should be so sharp that the eye navigates without instruction, the way a well-designed building moves you through it without signage. Comprehension should be reflex.
>
> Reject everything that signals "generated": the generic card layouts, the default border radii, the meaningless gradients, the polite whitespace that exists because no one decided what should go there. Instead, build something that looks like it emerged from a studio where people argue about touch targets and have strong feelings about interaction latency.

## Phrase bank

Each axis points a direction. Foreign and literary phrases tagged.

**The work itself**

- make it sing
- spend it all
- burn at it
- make it shimmer
- follow the flash
- fail better (Beckett)

**Move the audience**

- make it land
- leave them stunned / speechless
- stop them cold
- move them
- pity and terror (Aristotle)

**Reach and ambition**

- reach for greatness
- a man's reach should exceed his grasp (Browning)
- not because they are easy, but because they are hard (JFK)
- per aspera ad astra: through hardship to the stars

**Stake your reputation**

- sign it
- put your name on it
- carve it in stone / carve it in the stars
- exegi monumentum (Horace, more lasting than bronze)

**Become the reference**

- make it the benchmark
- set the bar
- set a standard

**Integrity and wholeness**

- make it true
- nothing false in it
- only connect (Forster)
- make it new (Pound)

**Breath and the human hand**

- make it deeply human
- make it breathe
- give it a pulse
- wabi-sabi: beauty in the imperfect, impermanent, incomplete (Japanese)

**Commit, no retreat**

- cross the Rubicon / the die is cast (Caesar)
- burn the boats (Cortés)
- once more unto the breach (Shakespeare)

**Craft against excess**

- nothing missing, nothing wasted
- murder your darlings (Quiller-Couch)
- sprezzatura: the art that hides effort (Castiglione)

Outward phrases push at the audience or the record. Integrity phrases pull inward, the work answering to itself. Pair the two when ambition needs ballast.

## Greenfield versus retrofit

The long form assumes a blank canvas. To raise an existing thing instead, trim the lines that insist on starting over:

- "I don't care about what but about how": drop it, the subject exists.
- "Build this": soften to "push this until" or "find the version of this that."
- The enemies list: keep only the tells the existing piece shows.
